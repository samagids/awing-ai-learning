## Full Description (4000 chars max)
Awing AI Learning is a free, interactive language learning app designed to teach the Awing language — a Grassfields Bantu language spoken by about 19,000 people in the Mezam division, North West Province, Republic of Cameroon.

Built for kids and beginners, the app features colorful lessons, fun quizzes, and AI-powered pronunciation practice across three difficulty levels.

BEGINNER LEVEL
Learn the Awing alphabet (22 consonants and 9 vowels), everyday vocabulary across categories like animals, food, family, and body parts, basic tone awareness, counting numbers, and common greetings and phrases. Practice pronunciation with speech recognition and hear words spoken aloud.

MEDIUM LEVEL
Explore consonant clusters (prenasalized, palatalized, and labialized), noun class patterns with singular and plural forms, sentence construction with word-by-word breakdowns, and vowel and syllable structure. Test your knowledge with writing quizzes.

EXPERT LEVEL
Master advanced tone identification, long and short word forms (elision rules), consonant allophonic rules, and Awing conversation practice with role-play dialogues. Challenge yourself with the expert quiz.

STORIES MODE
Read original stories in Awing with sentence-by-sentence progression, English translations, vocabulary highlights, and comprehension quizzes.

KEY FEATURES
- AI-powered pronunciation practice with speech recognition
- Six character voices (Bantu neural TTS) for natural-sounding pronunciation
- Spaced repetition system for vocabulary review
- Progress tracking with XP, levels, and achievement badges
- Daily streak tracking to build consistent habits
- Kid-friendly design with large buttons and encouraging feedback
- Dark mode support
- Works offline — no internet required after download
- Contribute new words and corrections to help grow the app

ABOUT THE AWING LANGUAGE
Awing is a tonal language with three pitch levels (high, mid, low) plus rising and falling tones. It has a rich consonant system including prenasalized stops and palatalized clusters. The app's linguistic content is based on published orthography and phonology research.

Created by Dr. Guidion Sama, DIT, to help preserve and promote the Awing language for future generations.
